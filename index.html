Each of these dots is a death from vaccine preventable diseases. At least, 7,131 people died every day on average in 2017 Almost two in five of them were children under 5 years old But this is just one day. Around 2.6 million died worldwide that year The number has halved since 1990, from 5 million, with much of the drop coming since 2000 Pneumonia caused by the pneumococcus bacteria and rotavirus are the main causes of death Measles, Hib and tetanus have also killed millions, but the number dying has fallen substantially In recent years some vaccine preventable diseases have been on the rise Measles deaths, for example, have doubled in Europe in the last decade, from 86 to 193
